# ☕ Coffee Sales Dashboard Analysis

## 📌 Business Problem  
The business aims to analyze coffee sales performance across different roast types, customer segments, countries, and time periods. Stakeholders (sales managers, marketing teams, and business owners) need insights into which products perform best, who the top customers are, and where sales are strongest. The goal is to make data-driven decisions to increase revenue, improve customer retention, and optimize product strategy.

---

## 📊 Dataset  
- **Source:** Coffee sales dataset (Excel / internal sales data)  
- **Size:** ~2–3 years of transactional data  
- **Time Period:** 2019 – 2022 (monthly data)  

### Key Variables:
- Order Date  
- Roast Type (Dark, Medium, Light)  
- Coffee Type (Arabica, Robusta, Liberica, Excelsa)  
- Customer Name  
- Country  
- Loyalty Card (Yes/No)  
- Product Size (0.2kg, 0.5kg, 1kg, 2.5kg)  
- Sales Amount (₹)

---

## 🛠 Tools & Technologies  
- **Excel** (Dashboard creation, slicers, charts)  
- Data Cleaning & Transformation  
- Data Visualization Techniques  

---

## ❓ Key Questions Answered  
1. Which coffee type generates the highest sales over time?  
2. Who are the top 5 customers contributing to revenue?  
3. Which country generates the highest sales?  
4. How do roast types and product sizes impact sales?  
5. What are the sales trends and seasonal patterns over time?  

---

## 🔍 Key Findings  
- Arabica coffee shows the most consistent and highest sales trend over time  
- A small group of top customers contributes significantly to total revenue  
- The United States generates the highest sales compared to other countries  
- Sales fluctuate monthly, indicating seasonal demand patterns  
- Medium and Dark roast types perform better than Light roast  

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations  
1. Focus on high-performing coffee types like Arabica  
2. Strengthen loyalty programs to retain top customers  
3. Expand business in the United States market  
4. Plan inventory based on seasonal trends  
5. Promote underperforming products using offers and bundles
